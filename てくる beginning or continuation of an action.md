# てくる　beginning or continuation of an action.

## **1. b**egin to: indicates change てくる

**or requires some time to complete　change in the future** 

1. 留学生の数が**増えてくる。**"The number of students from overseas begins to increase."
2. 最近 暖かく**なってきた**。"It's become warm recently."
3. 僕の彼女は**最近太ってきた**。"My girlfriend has begun to gain weight lately."
4. 結婚してから、僕の髪の毛は**少なくなってきている**。"I've started losing my hair since I got married." (literally, "My hair is beginning to become less after my marriage.")

## 2. **have been.　てきた**

up to the current point of time.    have been -changing in the past  "have been -ing”

**For Example:**

1. ずっと日本語を勉強**してきました**。 "I've studied Japanese right up until now. (literally, "I've been studying Japanese the whole time up until this point.")
2. *Jū-ni nen kan kono kaisha de **hataraite kita**.*12年間この会社で**働いてきた**。"He's worked at this company for twelve years." (literally, "He's been working at this company for twelve years up until this point.")

To form a negative sentence that means "haven't been -ing," change the *kuru* part into the negative.

**For Example:**

1. 今まで、全然勉強**してきませんでした**。"I haven't been studying at all up until now."
2. *Mattaku hon o yonde **konakatta**.*全く本を読んで**こなかった**。"I haven't been reading books at all (up until now)."
