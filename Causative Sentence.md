# to make someone do something

**"At this school, do you make the students do the cleaning?"**

**この学校では生徒に掃除させているんですか。**

**What's a Causative Sentence?**

Causative sentences express the idea that ***someone causes or allows someone to do something***. It corresponds to "make" as in "My parents always make me do my homework," "have" as in "I'll have him call later," or "let" as in "I'll let you win the game this time" in English.

## **How to Make the Causative Forms of Verbs**

* Class 1 Verb → Add **せる**　to **ない**　form stem
* Class 2 Verb → Add **させる**　 to **ます**　stem
* Class 3 Verb → する⇒ させる, *kuru* ⇒ *kosaseru*

## **How to Make Causative Sentences**

**■ Particle usage**

* The particle は　usually marks the causer.
* **に／を****marks the **causee*.***

**■  Formation**

When the verb has an object, the particle *ni* marks the cause or the person who is performing the action. In this lesson, we'll focus on this usage.

**[causer] *wa* [causee] *ni* [object] *o* [causative form of a verb]**

先生は生徒達に本を読ませて、感想文を**書かせた**。

友達をカラオケに連れて行って、歌を**歌わせた**

**●　Permissive causative**

私は息子**に**アメリカに行かせた。"I let my son go to the United States."

**●　Coercive causative**

私は息子**を**アメリカに行かせた。"I **made** my son go to the United States."
