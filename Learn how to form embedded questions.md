# Learn how to form embedded questions

**The Focus of This Lesson Is Forming Embedded Questions.**

**気に入るかどうかわからないけど。**

An embedded question is basically **a question that is inside another statement** or question

## **Embedded "Yes/No" Questions**

To insert a "yes/no" question into another sentence, attach **かどうか at the end of an informal sentence.** 

Please note that we **omit the だending of adjective or noun sentences.**

**■ *ki ni iru* (気に入る) "to like," "to be pleased with," "to take a fancy"**

There are a lot of idioms that contain the word　*ki*(気), meaning "spirit," "mind," or "mood" in Japanese, and this　*ki ni iru*　(気に入る) is one of them. The idiom with the opposite meaning is

*ki ni iranai*　(気に入らない).

**■ Counter suffixes**

*haku*(泊 ): Counter for overnight stays

*nichi*(日): Counter for number of days (duration)

*hon*(本): Counter for long cylindrical objects

*fun*(分): Counter for minutes

### **Cultural Insights**

***Otoshi dama* (お年玉) "New Year's money gift"**

There is a custom of adults giving the children of their relatives money during the New Year's season. This gift of money, which is known as *otoshidama,* is handed out in a small decorated envelope. How much money is given depends on the family and the child's age.
